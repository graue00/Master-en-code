History and facts about Bandera County TX Bandera County starts 25 mi northwest of San Antonio. It belongs to the Edwards Plateau region of southwest Texas. North of Bandera County is Kerr County and Kendall county. East lies Bexar County, South are Medina County and Uvalde County. West of Bandera County Real County is located. Bandera County is 792 square miles of rolling hills, scenic open land, magnificent cypress and cedar trees, lakes and rivers, with elevations that range from 1,200 to 2,300 feet. The western part of the county is drained by the Sabinal River and the eastern part by the Medina River. The Medina River runs from the northwest corner of Bandera County southeast into Medina Lake.
